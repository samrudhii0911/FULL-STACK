sachin tendulkar

Sachin Tendulkar, widely regarded as one of the greatest cricketers in the history of the sport, is an Indian former international cricketer who played for the Indian national team from 1989 to 2013. Born on April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, India, Tendulkar is known for his exceptional batting skills, impeccable technique, and unmatched consistency. He holds numerous records in international cricket, including being the highest run-scorer in both Test and One Day Internationals (ODIs). Tendulkar was the first player to score 100 international centuries and the first to reach 200 runs in a single ODI innings. Fondly called the "Little Master" and the "Master Blaster," he inspired generations of cricketers and fans worldwide. In 2011, he fulfilled a lifelong dream by winning the ICC Cricket World Cup with India. After retiring in 2013, he was honored with the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, making him the youngest recipient at the time. His legacy extends beyond cricket, as he is also involved in philanthropy and continues to be a respected voice in the cricketing world.
